Business Logistics Checklist for Nigerian SMEs

A practical logistics checklist for Nigerian SMEs covering route planning, receiver confirmation, inventory readiness, proof-of-delivery and escalation contacts.

Discuss this article Back to insights

SMEs that want to serve bigger clients need a logistics process that looks organised before volume increases. A simple checklist can protect customer trust and reduce delivery surprises.

Prepare the movement request

Each request should capture item description, quantity, pickup point, delivery address, receiver details and delivery priority.

Set escalation contacts

When delivery hits traffic, address confusion or receiver unavailability, someone must be available to make a decision quickly.

Track proof and exceptions

Proof-of-delivery and exceptions should be recorded. This helps businesses review recurring issues and improve future movement.

Review recurring routes

Frequent delivery areas should be studied for timing, cost and risk. Recurring patterns can improve planning.
